Walls

Wall measurements are one of the most critical parts of the estimate. You will trace both interior and exterior walls in AutoCAD to calculate total linear footage for framing, sheetrock, and other trades. It is important that you utilize the Ceiling Framing Layout from the PDF plan drawing for this section as well.

Regular Walls & Knee Walls

Regular walls are standard full-height walls. Knee walls are shorter walls — commonly found in attic spaces, bonus rooms, and at kitchen bars. Both must be measured accurately because they affect framing lumber and sheetrock quantities.

  • Do not double-count walls that are shared between rooms.

Note

When ceiling joists from a 10' room fall on an adjacent 9' wall, a 1' knee wall would be needed to make up the difference. When ceiling joists from a 10' room do not land on an adjacent 9' wall, you will go with the higher ceiling height of the two. This is why it is important to pay close attention to the ceiling height callouts in each room. (Move this note below the 'Knee Walls CAD' image)

Note

In rooms where there are tray ceilings, always honor the actual ceiling height. Ignore the height called out for the tray ceiling.
