5/4x4x12 Cement Smooth Trim

This is needed for windows, doors, vents, garages, corners, and beams.

  • In AutoCAD, use LINE to draw on the perimeter of windows, doors, and vents. For the sides, trace inside of the trim. For the tops and bottoms, trace outside of the trim. For windows with less than 6” of separation between them, treat the perimeter as one window.
  • When finished, pull those lines out and rotate your vertical lines to horizontal.
  • Draw a white 12x15 rectangle and place your lines in the rectangle one on top of the other, equal to what you did for crown and shelving. The goal is the same: for the lines to make runs that do not exceed 12', or the width of the rectangle. You will be able to connect some lines together to try to get as close to 12' as possible. If you have more than one color, be sure to group your lines by color.
  • Count the lines in the rectangle. Add 1 for each inside corner. Add 2 for each outside corner.

6 1/4x12 Smooth Fiber Cement Siding

This is for lapped siding only.
  • Use the Total SF Lap siding calculated previously and multiply it by 1.1. Divide by the exposure, which is typically 5''.
  • Round to the next whole number and enter into the spreadsheet.
